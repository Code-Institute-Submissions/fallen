Tell Us Your Story Anonymously

My Habit/Problem/Addiction

Voicing what is really going for us, without fear of being judged or rejected or social services arriving, can release the pain, shame and longing to be understood. Human connection is the perfect antidote to many illnesses. Physical, mental, emotional and spiritual illnesses heal if we find a way of expressing matters that are important to us. Recent studies show that connection is an effective way to tackle addiction. Perhaps a structured thought process can dip in to the recesses of our minds to lead us out of isolation and the shadows. Beating to the sound of our drum means recognising who we are and who we want to be not what we think others expect us to be.

Self Acceptance starts with tackling habits and addictions that are stunting or ruining our lives.

Addiction is a psychological and physical inability to stop consuming a chemical, drug, activity, or substance, even though it is causing psychological and physical harm.
