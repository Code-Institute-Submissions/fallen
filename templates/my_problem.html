Early days- For anyone trying recovery for less than 3 months

Tell Your Story- Anonymously
Human connection is the perfect antidote to many illnesses.

Voicing what is really going for us, without fear of being judged or rejected or of social services arriving, can release the pain and shame of addiction. Longing to be understood and a disconnected feeling can make life seem unbearable. Recent studies show that connection is an effective way to tackle addiction. Physical, mental, emotional and spiritual illnesses heal if we find a way of expressing matters that are important to us. This means, beating to the sound of our drum means recognising who we are and who we want to be not what we think others expect us to be is crucial for happiness in recovery.

Self Acceptance starts with tackling habits and addictions that are stunting or ruining our lives.

Addiction is a psychological and physical inability to stop consuming a chemical, drug, activity, or substance, even though it is causing psychological and physical harm.
